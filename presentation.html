Geothermal & Seismic Dashboards
Presentation (Frontend-only)

What we built

Two web dashboards to understand how plant operations relate to small earthquakes. Everything runs in the browser (HTML/CSS/JS only).

Open Operational Dashboard ↗ Open Risk Dashboard ↗

Goal (in simple words)

  • See plant activity over time (flows, pressures, temperature, energy).
  • See when earthquakes happened and how strong they were.
  • Compare both to spot patterns and reduce risk.

The two datasets

  • Seismic events: date/time, magnitude, location, phase → ~380 events.
  • Operational metrics: frequent readings (flows, pressures, temperature, energy, phase) → large time series.

We align them by time to compare operations vs earthquakes.

How to read the main chart

  • Blue/green lines = injection flow and injection pressure.
  • Dots = earthquakes (size/color can reflect magnitude).
  • Colored bands = operating phases.
  • Right axis can show probability (pTLS) for risk.

Zoom, pan, reset, and toggle series with the checkboxes to explore.

What the data tells us

  • Most events occur during or shortly after higher injection periods.
  • Setting flow to 0 when not producing makes patterns clear.
  • Pressures help explain when small quakes are more likely.

How to demo (2 minutes)

  • Open the Operational dashboard → change date range → show KPIs update.
  • Toggle inj_flow and inj_whp → point out seismic dots.
  • Open Risk dashboard → show probability curves and thresholds.
